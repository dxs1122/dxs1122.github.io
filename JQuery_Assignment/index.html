How To Make Fast Food Burgers at Home

By David Sneider

Having been in quarantine for over a month without being able to eat out, it is hard not to crave a good burger.

I'm here to tell you that you can make burgers in your own home that taste like they come from In N' Out, McDonalds, Shake Shack etc (but are tastier and more fresh).

Sauce

Step 1? Yes! No good burger is complete without a good sauce.

Here is my favorite recipe. It is like an elevated In 'n Out or Big Mac sauce. You will want to put it on everything. Exact measurements are given, but not necessary.

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped pickles
  • 2 teaspoons of vinegar (add more if you don't have pickles)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparing the Meat

Believe it or not, it is possible to make juicy, crispy burgers in minutes. What's the key?

  1. Preferably, use 80/20 lean/fat ground beef
  2. Form golf-ball-sized patties without over-stressing the meat to ensure maximum tenderness/juicyness.
  3. Crucial: Season both side of the patties liberally! This ensures--through the power of the Maillard Reaction--that they form a nice crust. To those who are health concious, much of this seasoning will disappear after cooking.

Time to Cook!

Remember: Great burgers can take minutes. How? Smash-burger style!

  1. On high heat, put a minimal amount of oil in a pan. Before it reaches its peak heat for the meat, put the buns on to toast.
  2. Once the pan has reached peak heat, take buns off and place patties on.
  3. Take two spatulas and flatten/smash the patties. Each side will take around 3-4 minutes to cook.
  4. Once one side is cooked and you flip the burgers, if you desire cheese, place it on the cooked side of the patties and cover the them with a lid so that the cheese steams.

Veggie Option-Portobello Burger

Let's not forget about veggie-lovers...Even Shake Shack serves a Portobello burger...

  1. Cut off the stems of the mushrooms to create a burger shape.
  2. Portobellos taste fine by themselves, but marinating them in olive oil and balsamic vinegar for 15-30 minutes at room temperature will deeply enhances their flavor and texture.
  3. Preheat a skillet on medium heat with a decent amount olive oil and brown both sides of the mushroom, like a beef burger, for about 6 minutes each side.
  4. Season afterwards with salt and pepper.

Extras?

You have your bun, your sauce, your meat/mushroom and your bun. For some, that's good enough.

But what if you want In 'n Out "animal style"? Add carmelized onions by cooking onions in a pan at a low heat for at least 30 minutes.

But what if you miss the taste of a Big Mac? Double up on meat, add one extra bun.

The list goes on and on.

Have fun!
